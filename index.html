Interest in IT

I have been interested in IT for as long as I can remember. I used to play with computers from a young age, taking an interest in coding and how systems work. I had a Commodore, Spectrum ZX, Atari ST and an Amiga before I got my first x86 computer. Installing Windows 95 from around 26 floppy disks was a personal achievement... In my teens I used to build PCs for family and friends as I enjoyed taking things apart and getting them running.
I started working in IT in 2001 on the helpdesk looking after a bank of modems that connected our dealer network to the mainframe. That job involved a lot of turning things off and on again! I've stayed in IT since then across a few industries, working for tech vendors for the last five years.
I currently am really interested by how companies are being disrupted by technology. With technologies such as cloud, the barrier to entry is much lower. A startup can have global reach with hardly any capital investment. Companies such as Netflix and Stan have risen from this and the demise of more traditional organisations that didnt adapt, such as Blockbuster are the result. Moving forward now, there is a situation where we are hitting the limits of streaming services with competition and these companies that were disruptors, now have to reinvent themselves to stay ahead. Another great example is Uber and the taxi industry, that in addition to the technolgy challenges, have brought a number of ethical challenges with its operation.

I chose to come to RMIT as I was looking at a course I could study online with a good reputation with an interesting variety of topics. I struggle to have fixed time periods that are available as I travel quite a lot, so this seems to fit well into that.

My background is in the infrastructure space; servers, storage, networking, security etc. Whilst I have some knowledge around systems and coding, its far from my area of specialism. I'm hoping to gsain a greater appreciation of this space and side of IT through the course.
